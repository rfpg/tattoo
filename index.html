Bran & Sceólang

Tattoo design brief for a horizontal, Hiberno-Saxon inspired composition focused on Fionn mac Cumhaill's two hounds. This page is self-contained and ready for GitHub Pages.

Design direction

Concept: Bran and Sceólang in a manuscript-inspired Insular style, with the composition carried by their bodies, paws, tails, and surrounding leaves rather than by a central symbol.

Character balance: Bran sits on the left with darker visual weight; Sceólang sits on the right, slightly lighter and a touch more lifted in posture.

Mood: Tense but not chaotic, alive, noble, mythic, and readable at a distance.

Refined brief

Client-facing summary
  • Two hounds only, Bran on the left, Sceólang on the right.
  • Horizontal composition with manuscript-style leaves woven around and beneath them.
  • Hiberno-Saxon / Insular influence without becoming too knot-dense.
  • Bran darker with emerald eye; Sceólang lighter with lapis eye.
  • Muted gold and scarlet accents; leaf green may pop more brightly.
Tattooer notes
  • Primary outline bold and stable; internal structure medium weight.
  • Keep negative space between paws, heads, and tail spirals.
  • Avoid dense center fill or unreadable knot masses.
  • Use skin breaks for highlights where possible.
  • Patterning should support the silhouette, not overpower it.
What to avoid
  • No central logo or knot dominating the design.
  • No wrap-to-chest composition.
  • No over-symmetrized logo stiffness.
  • No tight micro-detail that will blur over time.

Quick copy block

This is the exact text used by the copy button.

Bran and Sceólang in a Hiberno-Saxon / Insular manuscript style, focused entirely on the two hounds. Horizontal composition preferred. No Dara knot or central logo. Bran on the left: darker, black with grey patterning, emerald green eye. Sceólang on the right: lighter, grey/white with black interspersed, lapis blue eye. Palette is primarily black and grey with restrained Insular accents: muted gold, scarlet red, and brighter green leaves. Tongues should remain a natural pink/red. The design should stay breathable, readable, and tattooable, with leaves included and enough negative space for longevity.
